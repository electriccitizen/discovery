### What we currently understand

- The current site (`educationusa.state.gov`) is Drupal 7. We do not have backend or database access; the LOA committed migration via "scraping tools."
- EC will produce a sitemap-derived URL inventory from the crawl. Reconciling that raw inventory against what should actually migrate vs. drop vs. rebuild requires editorial judgment from IIE — REAC network, content owners, anyone with knowledge of what each page is for and whether it belongs on the new site. EC can't make those calls from crawl data alone.
- Some content can't be reached by the scrape and requires IIE to supply source files directly. Known categories: the gated PDFs (Regional Fact Sheets, Global Guide) and the gated/portal-only content (Region landing pages, Webinars, Campus News Stories). For each, source comes from a Drive/Box export, the current-site file directory, or a REAC-login export — we'll need IIE to coordinate.
- **The bigger concern is gated content with no source file to export.** Without a DB dump (B1), several categories on the current site can't be migrated at all — they'd need IIE to re-author manually on the new site. We've identified the broad buckets but **we don't know exactly what's in any of them** from outside the gate: the 12 Region landing pages (L12), an unknown number of portal-only Campus News Stories (L3), Adviser Profiles if the portal holds them (L6), Webinars content (L8), and the ~778 truly orphaned `/node/N` URLs (L7) that we can't classify at all. Rough combined volume guess: a few hundred pages, but that's a guess — accurate sizing requires IIE input from someone with portal access. A DB dump resolves most of it; without one, manual recreation is the only path.
- **Taxonomy terms and content tagging can't be migrated from a scrape alone.** We can see term *labels* from filter dropdowns (the 12 Region terms, 177 Country terms, 5 Degree Levels, etc.) but not the term IDs, hierarchy, custom fields, or — critically — which content items are tagged with which terms. Without a DB dump (B1), we rebuild vocabularies from the observed labels and re-tag content during migration; any term-level metadata invisible on the surface is lost. Worth noting: the current site has two parallel country representations (a Drupal taxonomy with 177 terms used by Centers/Scholarships, plus the address module's 250 ISO codes used by Events) — reconciling those is much cleaner with DB access.
- **Setting expectations on manual recreation.** Some content always needs to be rebuilt by hand in any migration — whether because it's gated and not exportable, because the structured shape on the new site introduces fields the old site never had, or because IIE's editorial review surfaces content that needs a fresh write rather than a port. We work with you to minimize this (a DB dump, where obtainable, dramatically shrinks it), but anything that can't be migrated cleanly becomes IIE's to rebuild on the new site. Concrete examples we already foresee: the gated content listed above; **new fields added to existing content types** (e.g., a country tag on Success Stories per E4(c), region metadata for Adviser Profiles per L6); and content categories that change shape between old and new. We'll flag these as we go so the editorial workload is visible up-front, not a surprise at launch.
- The current sitemap contains **8,055 URLs** total. Most of that volume is historical: ~5,624 Event records (likely accumulated over many years; the public finder shows only upcoming), ~857 Scholarship records (vs. ~270 currently active), and ~965 unaliased `/node/N` URLs (mostly 403-gated internal content). The substantive content surface to actually migrate is roughly **~5,000 URLs** once historical events and stale records are filtered — though final migrate/drop calls per page still need IIE editorial review.
- Four structured content types are visible on the public site: advising centers (~422 records), scholarships (the `/find-financial-aid` View is the same Scholarship type re-presented; ~270 active records via the finder), events, and a video library (~242 records). Video as a content medium is LOA-committed; the question is whether the new build preserves the current site's dedicated video library pattern or folds video content into other types — see §L2.

### Questions for you

**B1. Final effort on obtaining a D7 database dump.** We don't need backend access, accounts etc. — just an export of the database. Is there a possibility?
- *Why it matters:* Working from public HTML alone, we lose structured nuance (taxonomy IDs, entity references, admin-only fields, draft state). An export materially improves migration quality and de-risks the timeline.
- *Expected format:* a named contact, "we'll escalate via ECA," or "confirmed not obtainable."

**B2. How will IIE provide editorial review on the migrate/drop decisions?**
- *Why we ask:* The Q&A indicated IIE was producing a content inventory in parallel. We'd like to confirm the current status of that work — and in any case, EC will need editorial-review time from REAC / content owners / IIE staff to reconcile what should actually migrate vs. drop vs. rebuild. What's the right mechanism and cadence for that review work?
- *Expected format:* a named owner + a working pattern (e.g., weekly review session, batch sign-offs via Drive, REAC-network polling).

**B3. Beyond the gated content we've already identified, are there other hidden pages, downloads, or asset libraries we should know about?** Our crawl + redirect-pass surfaced a substantial gated surface: the 12 Region landing pages (L12), the Country Fact Sheets library (L9), the Submit Content portal, and Campus News / Adviser Profile content that appears to live in the portal (L3, L6). What we can't see from outside the gate is anything that's not linked or hinted at on the public side — orphan content, internal-only PDF dumps in unusual directory paths, separate file-storage areas. Does your network (advisers, REACs, IIE staff) know of any?
- *Why we ask:* Orphan content and PDF dumps in unusual paths often live alongside the public site without being linked. Better to surface them now than discover them post-migration.

**B4. Does IIE agree with the overall migration strategy and risks?** To summarize: EC scrapes the public site, produces a URL inventory, and migrates structured content where we can. For everything else — gated content, taxonomies invisible to a crawl, content changing shape on the new build — IIE supplies source files where they exist, provides editorial judgment on what to migrate, and recreates content manually where there's no other path. A DB dump (B1) materially reduces the manual-recreation surface; without it, manual recreation expands.
- *Why we ask:* The overall division of labor is foundational and affects timeline and IIE's editorial workload. Better to confirm alignment now than discover misalignment at content-cutover time.
- *Expected format:* "yes, proceed as described" / "yes, with these changes: …" / "let's discuss."
