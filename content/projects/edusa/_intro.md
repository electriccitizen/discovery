## Welcome to the Discovery Portal

This portal collects the questions we need answered before the build can move past the Strategy & Analysis phase. It replaces the original Google-Doc-style worksheet with something interactive: you respond inline, we comment inline, and we both watch progress.

### How the worksheet is organized

The structure mirrors the technical work streams of the project. Each section starts with **what we currently understand** — so you know the assumptions we're operating from — then asks **specific questions** we need answers to. Each question explains *why we're asking* so you know what we'll do with the answer.

### How to use the portal

- **Type your response** in the box on each question — it saves automatically as you type.
- Use the **Status** pills to mark your progress (Not started / In progress / Answered / Needs clarification).
- The **comment thread** under each question is where we can have a back-and-forth: clarifications, push-back, follow-ups, anything.
- If a question doesn't apply or you don't know yet, leave a comment like "unknown" or "let's discuss" — we'll take it up live.
- **Reference documents** (sitemap inventory, migration analysis, technical analysis, etc.) are available from the **Reference documents** menu at the top right of every page.

### Important context

- This worksheet covers **technical/architectural questions only**. Content strategy, design direction, and brand are handled in separate streams.
- Our questions are based on the signed LOA, the RFP, the RFP Q&A, and our preliminary exploration of the existing site at `educationusa.state.gov`. Where the LOA and Q&A diverged, we've flagged the divergence for your input.
- A handful of items are flagged where the LOA scope and your earlier Q&A guidance pointed in different directions — those are explicit decision points, not assumptions.
