### What we currently understand

- The current site is static HTML with no database or CMS behind it, so there's nothing to migrate automatically. Per the Q&A, migration is manual and not one-to-one.
- Our role is to produce a migration plan and set up the content types and configuration; your team does the manual content work.
- What to keep, rewrite, or drop is decided during Content Strategy (the inventory and audit). This section is about the migration plan and mechanics.

### Questions for you

**H1. How far back do you plan to go with the news migration? The site holds years of news (the current feed plus legacy district archives).**
- **Recommendation:** Migrate a recent window (say the last one to two years) and archive or drop the rest, rather than carrying thousands of old releases forward. Confirm the cutoff.
- *Why we ask:* News is the highest-volume content, so the cutoff drives the bulk of the migration effort.

**H2. For news, do you want us to recommend an assisted import rather than hand-entering everything? Is there other high-volume content to consider such as events?**
- **Recommendation:** If the current news HTML is consistent, a scripted import into the new content type can move the bulk of the content, with staff handling cleanup and the rest by hand. If there are other large, repetitive content sets, tell us and we'd plan the same approach.
- *Why we ask:* It can save significant effort if you plan to migrate a lot of the old news items.

**H3. What's the plan for PDFs and other documents? The site links to many (plans, maps, reports), and legacy document accessibility is out of scope per the Q&A.**
- **Recommendation:** Migrate only documents still in use, and set a rule for the rest (keep where actively linked, drop the orphaned or outdated). New or updated documents should meet accessibility standards, even though remediating old ones isn't in scope.
- *Why we ask:* Documents are a large share of the volume and need a clear keep/drop rule.
