### What we currently understand

- ~150 editors maintain content across nine districts and central offices, mostly in their own areas, with no formal review step before publishing.
- Drupal supports moderation states and scoped editorial access. The decisions here are how content gets reviewed and who can edit what.

### Questions for you

**B1. What review process (if any) do you want before content goes live (e.g. self-publish, or a review and approval step)?**
- **Recommendation:** A basic moderation workflow (Draft → Needs Review → Published → Archived), with publishing limited to the review and manager roles defined in section A.
- *Why we ask:* This shapes the workflow we recommend and which roles can publish.

**B2. Should editors be able to edit anywhere, or be limited to their district or office area?**
- **Recommendation:** Limit access by section/district areas using the Workbench Access module. This provides a method to control editorial access by groups or sections.
- *Why we ask:* This determines the editorial access model and modules we recommend.

**B3. Who owns content across the districts and central offices? Is there a central web team, or is it fully distributed across districts and offices?**
- **Recommendation:** Provide full content access to the central web team using the Site Manager role. Create a separate Section Manager role that provides content approval permissions per section.
- *Why we ask:* We need to know what level of distributed access works best for your team, vs. centralized control.
